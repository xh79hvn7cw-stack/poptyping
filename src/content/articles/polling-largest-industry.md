A new study from a major polling firm has determined that polling itself now accounts for 18.4% of national GDP, narrowly overtaking insurance and slightly trailing the production of more polls.

The study was funded by a consortium of polling firms and conducted via a poll of 1,200 likely poll respondents.

## Methodology

Researchers polled Americans on their relationship to polls, then polled them on their answers, then polled a separate group on how they felt about that. A margin of error of ±3.2% applies, except in cases where it doesn't.

Findings include that 73% of respondents believe the country is "on the wrong track," while a separate 73% believe the country is "on the right track." A footnote clarifies that these are the same 73%.

## Industry response

Polling-industry leaders welcomed the report. "For too long, our work has been invisible," said one analyst. "Now Americans can see — through polling — exactly how much polling we do."

Asked whether they planned to commission further studies, the analyst responded that a poll on the question was already in the field.
