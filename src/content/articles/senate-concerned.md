In a rare moment of unity, the Senate voted 97-3 on Monday to formally adopt an expression of concern as the official posture of the chamber for the remainder of the legislative session, with the three dissenting senators reportedly absent because they were already concerned about something else.

The resolution, titled the *Looking Concerned Across All Topics Act*, will require senators to maintain "a furrowed brow, slow head-shake, and one (1) audible sigh per twelve-minute period" during all committee hearings, town halls, and televised appearances. Compliance will be monitored by an independent panel of cable-news facial coaches.

## A long-overdue reform

Supporters hailed the bill as a major step forward for the institution's brand. "We've been doing this informally for decades," said one senior senator, while looking concerned. "It's about time we made it official."

> "The American people deserve elected officials who will look concerned, sound concerned, and — when budgets allow — schedule a hearing about being concerned."

The bill includes a provision for "performative concern overflow," allowing senators to outsource excess facial expressions to junior staff during particularly demanding news cycles. Industry analysts predict this could create up to forty new jobs.

## Critics unmoved

Critics of the bill argue that it doesn't go far enough. "Where is the funding for the *grave* concern?" asked one watchdog group. "Where is the line item for the *deeply troubling*? Where is the appropriation for the *let me be very clear about something*?"

The senator who introduced the bill responded by looking concerned, then dismissed the question.
