A prominent Washington think tank has published a comprehensive nine-hundred-page report on the state of American policy research, the central finding of which is that more money should be given to the think tank that wrote it.

The report, three years in development and funded by an anonymous donor with no stated interests, draws on more than two thousand interviews, a dozen working groups, and what the executive summary describes as "rigorous independent analysis of our own bank statements."

## Key findings

The report's recommendations are organized around four pillars: increased investment in research, increased investment in researchers, increased investment in the offices where research occurs, and a small administrative fee.

"The data is unambiguous," said the think tank's president. "We need approximately the amount of money that we currently lack."

A separate appendix proposes a public-private partnership in which the public provides the funding and the private think tank determines how it is spent.
